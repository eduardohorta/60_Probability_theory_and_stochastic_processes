proof of Borel-Cantelli 2


Let E denote the set of samples that are in Ai infinitely often. We want to show that the complement of E has probability zero.

As in the proof of Borel-Cantelli 1, we know that

Ec=⋃k=1∞⋂i=k∞Aic

where the superscript c means set complement. But for each k,

P⁢(∩i=kAic) =∏i=k∞P⁢(Aic)
=∏i=k∞(1-P⁢(Ai))

Here we use the assumption that the event Ai’s are independent. The inequality 1-a≤e-a and the assumption that the sum of P⁢(Ai) diverges together imply that

P⁢(∩i=kAic)≤exp⁡(-∑i=k∞P⁢(Ai))=0

Therefore Ec is a union of countable number of events, each of them has probability zero. So P⁢(Ec)=0.
