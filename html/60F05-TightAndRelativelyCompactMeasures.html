Tight and relatively compact measuresFernando Sanz

Definition 1.

Let ℳ={μi,i∈I} be a family of finite measures on the Borel subsets of a metric space Ω. We say that ℳ is tight iff for each ϵ>0 there is a compact set K such that μi⁢(Ω-K)<ϵ for all i. We say that ℳ is relatively compact iff each sequence in ℳ has a subsequence converging weakly to a finite measure on ℬ⁢(Ω).

If {Fi,i∈I} is a family of distribution functions, relative compactness or tightness of {Fi} refers to relative compactness or tightness of the corresponding measures.

Theorem.

Let {Fi,i∈I} be a family of distribution functions with Fi⁢(∞)-Fi⁢(-∞)<M<∞ for all i. The family is tight iff it is relatively compact.
