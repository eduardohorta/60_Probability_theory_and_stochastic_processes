proof of hitting times are stopping times for right-continuous processes


Let (ℱ)t∈𝕋 be a filtration (http://planetmath.org/FiltrationOfSigmaAlgebras) on the measurable space (Ω,ℱ), It is assumed that 𝕋 is a closed subset of ℝ and that ℱt is universally complete for each t∈𝕋.

Let X be a right-continuous and adapted process taking values in a metric space E and S⊆E closed. We show that

τ=inf⁡{t∈𝕋:Xt∈S}

is a stopping time. Assuming S is nonempty and defining the continuous function dS⁢(x)≡inf⁡{d⁢(x,y):y∈S}, then τ is the first time at which the right-continuous process Yt=dS⁢(Xt) hits 0.

Let us start by supposing that 𝕋 has a minimum element t0.

If ℙ is a probability measure on (Ω,ℱ) and ℱtℙ represents the completion (http://planetmath.org/CompleteMeasure) of the σ-algebra ℱt with respect to ℙ, then it is enough to show that τ is an (ℱtℙ)-stopping time. By the universal completeness of ℱt it would then follow that

{τ≤t}∈⋂ℙℱtℙ=ℱt

for every t∈𝕋 and, therefore, that τ is a stopping time. So, by replacing ℱt by ℱtℙ if necessary, we may assume without loss of generality that ℱt is complete with respect to the probability measure ℙ for each t.

Let 𝒯 consist of the set of measurable times σ:Ω→𝕋∪{∞} such that {σ<t}∈ℱt for every t and that σ≤τ. Then let σ* be the essential supremum of 𝒯. That is, σ* is the smallest (up to sets of zero probability) random variable taking values in ℝ∪{±∞} such that σ*≥σ (almost surely) for all σ∈𝒯.

Then, by the properties of the essential supremum, there is a countable sequence σn∈𝒯 such that σ*=supn⁡σn. It follows that σ*∈𝒯.

For any n=1,2,… set

σ1=inf⁡{t∈𝕋:t≥σ*,Yt<1/n}.

Clearly, σ1≤τ and, choosing any countable dense subset A of 𝕋, the right-continuity of Y gives

{σ1<t}=⋃s<t,s∈A{σ*<s,Ys<1/n}∈ℱt.

So, σ1∈𝒯, which implies that σ1≤σ* with probability one. However, by the right-continuity of Y, σ1>σ* whenever σ* is finite and Yσ*>1/n, so

ℙ(σ*<∞,Yσ*>0)≤∑nℙ(σ*<∞,Yσ*>1/n)=0.

This shows that Yσ*=0 and therefore σ*≥τ whenever σ*<∞. So, σ*=τ almost surely and τ∈𝒯 giving,

{τ≤t}={τ<t}∪{Yt=0}∈ℱt.

So, τ is a stopping time.

Finally, suppose that 𝕋 does not have a minimum element. Choosing a sequence tn→-∞ in 𝕋 then the above argument shows that

τn=inf⁡{t∈𝕋:t≥tn,Yt=0}

are stopping times so,

{τ≤t}=⋃n{τn≤t}∈ℱt

as required.
