properties of expected value


1) (normalization) Let X be almost surely constant random variable, i.e. Pr⁡{X=c}=1; then E⁢[X]=c.

2) (linearity) Let X, Y be random variables such that E⁢[|X|]<∞ and E⁢[|Y|]<∞ and let a, b be real numbers; then E⁢[|a⁢X+b⁢Y|]<∞ and E⁢[a⁢X+b⁢Y]=a⁢E⁢[X]+b⁢E⁢[Y].

3) (monotonicity) Let X, Y be random variables such that Pr⁡{X≤Y}=1 and E⁢[|X|]<∞, E⁢[|Y|]<∞; then E⁢[X]≤E⁢[Y].

Proof.

1) Let’s define

F={ω∈Ω:X⁢(ω)=c};

Then by hypothesis

Pr⁡{Ω\F}=0

and

Pr⁡{F}=1.

We have:

E⁢[X] = ∫ΩX⁢(ω)⁢𝑑P
= ∫Ω\FX⁢(ω)⁢𝑑P+∫FX⁢(ω)⁢𝑑P
= ∫FX⁢(ω)⁢𝑑P
= ∫Fc⁢𝑑P
= c⁢Pr⁡{F}=c.

2) [to be done].

3) Let’s define

F={ω∈Ω:X⁢(ω)≤Y⁢(ω)};

Then by hypothesis

Pr⁡{Ω\F}=0

and

Pr⁡{F}=1.

We have, keeping in mind property 2),

E⁢[Y]-E⁢[X] = E⁢[Y-X]
= ∫Ω[Y⁢(ω)-X⁢(ω)]⁢𝑑P
= ∫Ω\F[Y⁢(ω)-X⁢(ω)]⁢𝑑P+∫F[Y⁢(ω)-X⁢(ω)]⁢𝑑P
= ∫F[Y⁢(ω)-X⁢(ω)]⁢𝑑P≥0.

∎
