proof of Martingale criterion


Let (τk)k≥1 be a localizing sequence of stopping times for X. Then:

Λ{τk≥n} ↑ΛΩ⁢a.s.⁢k→∞,∀n∈ℕ

since {τk≥n}↑k⋃1∞{τk≥n}∀n∈ℕ.

⋃k=1∞{τk≥n} =Ω⁢a.s., since⁢τk→∞,a.s.

Now assume E⁢Xn-<∞,∀n≥n0 (the case E⁢Xn+<∞ being analogous).

1) We have E⁢Xn-<∞⁢∀n∈ℕ.

We proceed by (backward) induction. For n=n0 the statement holds.

n↦n-1:

(Xτk)- =(Xτk∧n-)n∈ℕ⁢submartingale

We have:

∫{τk≥n}Xn-1-⁢𝑑P =∫{τk≥n}Xτk∧(n-1)-⁢𝑑P
≤∫{τk≥n}Xτk∧n-⁢𝑑P=∫{τk≥n}Xn-⁢𝑑P
≤∫Xn-⁢𝑑P<∞

Where the first to second line is the submartingale property and the last line follows by induction hypothesis.

Using Fatou we get:

∫Xn-1-⁢𝑑P =∫limk→∞⁡Xn-1-⁢Λ{τk≥n}⁢d⁢P
≤lim infk→∞⁡∫Xn-1-⁢Λ{τk≥n}⁢𝑑P
≤∫Xn-⁢𝑑P<∞

2) We have Xn∈ℒ1(n∈ℕ).

We have Xτk∧n+→Xn+ a.s., k→∞,∀n∈ℕ. With Fatou we get:

E⁢Xn+ ≤lim infk→∞⁡E⁢Xτk∧n+
=E⁢X0+lim infk→∞⁡E⁢Xτk∧n-
=E⁢X0+lim infk→∞⁡E⁢(∑j=0n-1Xj-⁢Λ{τk=j}+Xn-⁢Λ{τk≥n})
≤E⁢X0+∑j=0nE⁢Xj-<∞

With 1) Xn∈ℒ1 follows.

3)

X is a martingale, because Xnτk→Xn a.s. k→∞ and:

|Xnτk| ≤∑j=0n|Xj|∈ℒ1⁢(⁢ℒ1⁢-bound)

Thus Xnτk⟶L1Xn,k→∞⁢∀n∈ℕ by bounded convergence theorem. Hence X must be martingale and we are done. ∎
