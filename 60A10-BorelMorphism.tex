\begin{definition}
Let $\grp_B$ and $\grp_B$* be two groupoids whose object spaces are Borel. An \emph{algebraic morphism} from $\grp_B$
to $\grp_B$* is defined as a left action of $\grp_B$ on $\grp_B$* which commutes with the multiplication on $\grp_B$. Such an algebraic morphism between Borel groupoids is said to be a \emph{Borel morphism} if the action of $\grp_B$ on $\grp_B$* is Borel (viz. ref. \cite{MRB2k6})

\end{definition}
