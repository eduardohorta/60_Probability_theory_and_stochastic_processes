independent sigma algebras


Let (Ω,ℬ,P) be a probability space. Let ℬ1 and ℬ2 be two sub sigma algebras of ℬ. Then ℬ1 and ℬ2 are said to be if for any pair of events B1∈ℬ1 and B2∈ℬ2:

P⁢(B1∩B2)=P⁢(B1)⁢P⁢(B2).

More generally, a finite set of sub-σ-algebras ℬ1,…,ℬn is independent if for any set of events Bi∈ℬi, i=1,…,n:

P⁢(B1∩⋯∩Bn)=P⁢(B1)⁢⋯⁢P⁢(Bn).

An arbitrary set 𝒮 of sub-σ-algebras is mutually independent if any finite subset of 𝒮 is independent.

The above definitions are generalizations of the notions of independence (http://planetmath.org/Independent) for events and for random variables:

  1. 1.

    Events B1,…,Bn (in Ω) are mutually independent if the sigma algebras σ⁢(Bi):={∅,Bi,Ω-Bi,Ω} are mutually independent.

  2. 2.

    Random variables X1,…,Xn defined on Ω are mutually independent if the sigma algebras ℬXi generated by (http://planetmath.org/MathcalFMeasurableFunction) the Xi’s are mutually independent.

In general, mutual independence among events Bi, random variables Xj, and sigma algebras ℬk means the mutual independence among σ⁢(Bi), ℬXj, and ℬk.

Remark. Even when random variables X1,…,Xn are defined on different probability spaces (Ωi,ℬi,Pi), we may form the product (http://planetmath.org/InfiniteProductMeasure) of these spaces (Ω,ℬ,P) so that Xi (by abuse of notation) are now defined on Ω and their independence can be discussed.
