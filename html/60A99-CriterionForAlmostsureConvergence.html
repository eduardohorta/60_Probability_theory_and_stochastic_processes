criterion for almost-sure convergence


Let X1,X2,… and X be random variables. If, for every ϵ>0, the sum ∑n=1∞ℙ(|Xn-X|>ϵ) is finite, then Xn converge to X almost surely.

Proof.

By the Borel-Cantelli lemma, we have ℙ(lim supn{|Xn-X|>ϵ})=0. But lim supn{|Xn-X|>ϵ} is the same as the event {lim supn⁡|Xn-X|>ϵ}. (The latter event involves the limit superior of numbers (http://planetmath.org/LimitSuperior); the former involves the limit superior of sets (http://planetmath.org/InfinitelyOften).) So taking the limit ϵ↘0, we have ℙ(lim supn|Xn-X|>0)=0, or equivalently ℙ(lim supn|Xn-X|=0)=1. ∎
