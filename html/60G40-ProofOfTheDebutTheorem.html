proof of the début theorem


Let (ℱ)t∈𝕋 be a right-continuous filtration (http://planetmath.org/FiltrationOfSigmaAlgebras) on the measurable space (Ω,ℱ), It is assumed that 𝕋 is a closed subset of ℝ and that ℱt is universally complete for each t∈𝕋.

If A⊆𝕋×Ω is a progressively measurable set, then we show that its début

D⁢(A)=inf⁡{t∈𝕋:(t,ω)∈A}

is a stopping time.

As A is progressively measurable, the set A∩((-∞,t)×Ω) is ℬ⁢(𝕋)×ℱt-measurable. By the measurable projection theorem it follows that

{D(A)<t}={ω∈Ω:(s,ω)∈A∩((-∞,t)×Ω) for some s∈𝕋}

is in ℱt. If there exists a sequence tn∈𝕋 with tn>t and tn→t, then

{D(A)≤t}=⋂n{D(A)<tn}∈⋂nℱtn=ℱt+=ℱt.

On the other hand, if t is not a right limit point of 𝕋 then

{D(A)≤t}={D(A)<t}∪{ω∈Ω:(t,ω)∈A}∈ℱt.

In either case, {D⁢(A)≤t} is in ℱt, so D⁢(A) is a stopping time.
