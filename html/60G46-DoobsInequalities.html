Doob’s inequalities


Doob’s inequalities place bounds on the maximum value attained by a martingale in terms of the terminal value. We consider a process (Xt)t∈𝕋 defined on the filtered probability space (Ω,ℱ,(ℱ)t∈𝕋,ℙ). The associated maximum process (Xt*) is

Xt*≡sups≤t⁡|Xs|.

The notation ∥⋅∥p for the Lp-norm (http://planetmath.org/LpSpace) of a random variable will be used. In discrete-time or, more generally whenever the index set 𝕋 is countable, then Doob’s inequalities are as follows.

Theorem 1 (Doob).

Let (Xt)t∈T be a submartingale with countable index set T. Then,

ℙ(sups≤tXs≥K)≤K-1𝔼[(Xt)+] (1)

If X is either a martingale or nonnegative submartingale then,

ℙ(Xt*≥K)≤K-1𝔼[|Xt|], (2)
∥Xt*∥p≤pp-1⁢∥Xt∥p. (3)

for every K>0 and p>1.

In particular, (3) shows that the maximum of any Lp-bounded martingale is itself Lp-bounded and, martingales Xn converge to X in the Lp-norm if and only if (Xn-X)*→0 in the Lp-norm. The special case where p=2 gives

𝔼⁢[(Xt*)2]≤4⁢𝔼⁢[Xt2]

which is known as Doob’s maximal quadratic inequality.

Similarly, (2) shows that any L1-bounded martingale is almost surely bounded and that convergence in the L1-norm implies ucp convergence. Inequality (1) is also known as Kolmogorov’s submartingale inequality.

Doob’s inequalities are often applied to continuous-time processes, where 𝕋=ℝ+. In this case, Xt*=sups≤t⁡|Xs| is a supremum of uncountably many random variables, and need not be measurable. Instead, it is typically assumed that the processes are right-continuous, in which case, for any t>0 the supremum may be restricted to the countable set

𝕋′={s∈ℝ+:s/t∈ℚ}.

Putting this into Theorem 1 gives the following continuous-time version of the inequalities.

Theorem 2 (Doob).

Let (Xt)t∈R+ be a right-continuous submartingale. Then,

ℙ(sups≤tXs≥K)≤K-1𝔼[(Xt)]

for every K>0. If X is right-continuous and either a martingale or nonnegative submartingale then,

ℙ(Xt*≥K)≤K-1𝔼[|Xt|],
∥Xt*∥p≤pp-1⁢∥Xt∥p.

for every K>0 and p>1.
