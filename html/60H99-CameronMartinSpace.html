Cameron-Martin space


Definition 1.

Let W⁢(ℝd) be Wiener space. The Cameron-Martin space H⁢(ℝd) is the subspace of W⁢(ℝd) consisting of all paths ω such that ω is absolutely continuous and ∫0∞|ω′⁢(s)|2⁢𝑑s<∞. (Note that if ω is absolutely continuous, then it is almost everywhere differentiable, so the integral makes sense.)

This can be thought of as the set of paths with “finite energy.”

Note that H⁢(ℝd) has Wiener measure 0, since sample paths of Brownian motion are nowhere differentiable, whereas a path from H⁢(ℝd) is almost everywhere differentiable.
