\begin{Lem}
Let $X = (X_n, \mathcal{F}_n)$ be a local martingale. If there is an $n_0 \in \mathbb{N}$ such that $\forall \ n \geq n_0, n \in \mathbb{N}$ either:
\begin{align*}
E X_n^{-} &< \infty
\end{align*}

or:
\begin{align*}
E X_n^{+} &< \infty
\end{align*}

Then $X$ is a martingale.
\end{Lem}
