Feller process


Let E be a LCCB space (locally compact with a countable base; usually a subset of ℝn for some n∈ℕ) and C0⁢(E)=C0⁢(E,ℝ) be the space of continuous functions on E that vanish at infinity. (We may write C0 as shorthand.) A Feller semigroup on C0⁢(E) is a family of positive linear operators Tt,t≥0, on C0⁢(E) such that

  • •

    T0=I⁢d and ||Tt||≤1 for every t∈T, i.e. {Tt}t∈T is a family of contracting maps;

  • •

    Tt+s=Tt∘Ts (the semigroup property);

  • •

    limt↓0⁡||Tt⁢f-f||=0 for every f∈C0⁢(E).

A probability transition function associated with a Feller semigroup is called a Feller transition function. A Markov process having a Feller transition function is called a Feller process.
